Digital Image

Digital images are fundamentally a finite number of pixels distributed in X and Y axis, and are stored on the disk as a two (or more) dimentional array of numbers (or more complex datastructers) in rows and columns. Any 2D digital image can be viewed as a matrix of size NxM. Each element in the matrix represents a pixel. A pixel, its definition, and how a pixel is displayed on a screen or how it is printed out on a paper, is out of the context of this article.
